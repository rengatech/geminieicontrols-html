Energy Department Research Will Help Eagles Coexist with Wind Energy Deployment

As the demand for renewable energy grows, wind and solar power are increasingly being recognized as key solutions to achieving a sustainable future. However, the impact of wind energy on wildlife, such as eagles, has raised concerns, prompting the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) to conduct research aimed at helping eagles coexist with wind energy deployment. This research focuses on minimizing bird collisions with wind turbines, ensuring both wildlife protection and the continued expansion of clean energy sources.

The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) is advancing research to help eagles and wind energy facilities coexist harmoniously. As wind energy continues to grow as a sustainable power source, there is an increasing need to address concerns regarding wildlife protection, especially with large birds like eagles, which are at risk of colliding with wind turbines.
